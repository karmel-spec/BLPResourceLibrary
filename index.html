The profession's shared memory

For more than a century, piano technicians have accumulated extraordinary specialized knowledge — and most of it lives in individual shops, handwritten notes, and the minds of experienced techs. When a technician retires, those insights disappear.

"No piano technician should ever have to solve the same specialized problem twice."

This library preserves the geometry, the engineering, and the reasoning behind the parts — CAD models, jigs, manufacturing notes, and technique — as permanent digital assets for the whole profession.

Contribute to the library

The library is starting with the Brigham Larson Pianos shop collection and will grow deliberately: first as a public read-only library, then with invited contributors, then broader community participation.

Have a model, jig, or technique worth preserving? We'd love to add it — with full credit to you.
