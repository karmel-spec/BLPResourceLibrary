The fine print, in plain English

The Piano Technology Library is a free, community-run library where piano technicians share designs, files, and know-how. It works because people give and take in good faith. This page explains how that works, what we do and don't do, and the responsibilities that come with using it. Please read it — using the library means you agree to it.

Last updated July 24, 2026.

1. What this library is — and isn't

The vision is simple: make available new innovations and no-longer-available parts and piano-related items, so the craft's knowledge stays in the trade instead of disappearing when a shop closes. The library is a neutral meeting place where independent piano technicians post their own work and download others'.

It is not a store, a manufacturer, a publisher, or a certifying body. Brigham Larson Pianos operates the library as a community service and founding contributor — but the files, designs, videos, and services here come from many independent people, not from us.

2. We do not vet, review, or guarantee contributions

This is the most important thing to understand:

  • We do not review contributions for quality, accuracy, safety, or fitness for any purpose. A file being in the library is not an endorsement, a stamp of approval, or a promise that it will work, fit, or be safe.
  • We do not verify intellectual-property or legal rights. We do not check whether a design is patented, trademarked, copyrighted, or otherwise owned by someone else. We have no practical way to do so, and we don't try.
  • We do not test parts. Piano parts and tools can fail, break, or cause injury or instrument damage if they are poorly designed, wrong for the application, or improperly made. Nothing here has been tested or certified by us.

Everything in the library is provided "as is," with no warranties of any kind, express or implied, including any implied warranties of merchantability, fitness for a particular purpose, or non-infringement.

4. Contributors: only share work you have the right to share

Please do not upload proprietary designs that belong to others. The purpose of this library is to preserve obsolete, no-longer-available parts and to share your own innovations — not to redistribute someone else's protected intellectual property. When in doubt, leave it out, or ask the owner first.

You are responsible for verifying that anything you make or install is correct, safe, and appropriate for the instrument and situation in front of you. If you are not qualified to evaluate a part or technique, get help from someone who is.

6. Ratings and feedback make the community safer

Because no one is vetting quality for you, the community's own experience is the best guide. We strongly encourage you to:

  • Rate contributors and their contributions honestly;
  • Leave detailed, constructive feedback about what worked, what didn't, and what to watch out for;
  • Use others' ratings and feedback to decide which contributors and files to prioritize.

Abundant, honest feedback is how a use-at-your-own-risk community protects its members. Please contribute to it generously — and keep it fair, factual, and professional.
